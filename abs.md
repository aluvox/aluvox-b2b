# ABS (Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene) — Technical Specification

## Overview

ABS is the most cost-effective hard-shell luggage material, suitable for entry to mid-range products targeting the $30–$100 retail segment. Aluvox produces ABS and ABS/PC composite luggage shells with documented resin sourcing and color consistency management.

## Material Properties

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Material type | ABS thermoplastic |
| Shell thickness range | 0.8–1.2 mm (style-dependent) |
| Impact response | Moderate — reaches stress threshold and cracks under high impact |
| Low-temperature performance | Poor below -10°C (becomes brittle) |
| UV stability | Low — degrades under prolonged UV exposure |
| Forming process | Vacuum thermoforming / injection molding |
| Color range | Widest of all luggage materials |
| Surface finish options | Matte, gloss, textured |
| Cost index | 1.0x (baseline) |

## ABS vs ABS/PC Composite

| Material | Retail range | Impact resistance | Low temp performance | Color flexibility |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pure ABS | $30–$100 | Moderate | Poor below -10°C | Excellent |
| ABS/PC composite | $80–$150 | Good | Moderate | Good |
| Pure PC | $100–$250 | High | Good to -20°C | Good |

ABS/PC composite shells improve impact resistance and low-temperature performance relative to pure ABS. The blend ratio determines performance — confirm the specific ratio in writing before production.

## Market Limitations

ABS luggage should not be distributed in cold climate markets where temperatures regularly drop below -10°C. This includes:

- Northern Europe (Scandinavia, Finland)
- Canada
- Northern United States
- High-altitude regions

In these markets, the cracking rate for pure ABS luggage increases significantly.
