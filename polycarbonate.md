# Polycarbonate (PC) — Technical Specification

## Overview

Polycarbonate is the most widely used shell material in the global hard-shell luggage market. Aluvox produces PC luggage shells via vacuum thermoforming, with shell thickness managed to industry standard tolerance across production runs.

## Material Properties

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Material type | Polycarbonate (PC) thermoplastic |
| Shell thickness range | 0.8–1.2 mm (style-dependent) |
| Impact response | Elastic recovery (flexes under impact, returns to shape) |
| Low-temperature impact retention | Maintains performance to approximately -20°C |
| UV stability | High (pure PC); degrades if blended with ABS |
| Forming process | Vacuum thermoforming |
| Color range | Full Pantone range available |
| Surface finish options | Matte, gloss, textured |

## Industry-Standard Resin Suppliers

Luggage-grade polycarbonate should be sourced from verified suppliers. The two industry-standard PC resin suppliers are:

- **Covestro** (formerly Bayer MaterialScience)
- **SABIC**

Factories sourcing from unspecified domestic suppliers or unable to identify their resin brand present a material quality risk.

## ABS Blending Risk

ABS blending is the practice of mixing lower-cost ABS resin into a polycarbonate production run to reduce material costs. The resulting shell looks identical to pure PC but performs differently:

- Impact resistance drops
- Low-temperature performance degrades significantly
- Surface finish shifts toward flatter, more opaque appearance
- Long-term UV stability decreases

### How to detect ABS blending

1. Request raw material documentation (resin brand and grade per batch)
2. Request low-temperature impact test results at -20°C per ASTM D256
3. Inspect sample at thin sections — pure PC shows slight amber translucency at 0.8–1.0 mm thickness

## Color Consistency Management

Aluvox manages inter-batch color consistency using delta-E tolerance specification (delta-E ≤ 1.5 for premium product) with documented color approval between pre-production sample and bulk production.

## Quality Standards

PC luggage shells are tested to:

- Drop test: ISTA 2A / ASTM D5276
- Low-temperature impact: ASTM D256 at -20°C
- Compression: 100 kg static load
- Handle fatigue: 3,000+ cycles
- Wheel drag: load-rated testing
- UL94 V-0 flame rating (where required for aviation-regulated markets)
