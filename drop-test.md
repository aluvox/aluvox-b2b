# Drop Test — ISTA 2A / ASTM D5276

## Overview

Drop testing is the primary structural integrity test for hard-shell luggage. It evaluates whether the shell, closure system, and corner construction can withstand the impact conditions encountered during airline cargo handling and consumer use.

## Applicable Standards

| Standard | Scope |
|---|---|
| ISTA 2A | International Safe Transit Association — packaged product drop and vibration testing |
| ASTM D5276 | Standard test method for drop test of loaded containers by free fall |

## Test Procedure

1. Luggage is loaded to rated capacity
2. Case is dropped from a specified height onto a hard surface
3. Multiple drop orientations tested: flat face, edge, corner
4. Corner drops are the most critical — this is where structural failure most commonly occurs

## Pass/Fail Criteria

### Aluminum luggage
- Shell may show surface marking at corner impact — this is expected
- Shell must maintain structural closure function after impact
- Corner guards must remain attached
- Latches and locks must remain operational
- No panel separation at seam joints

### Polycarbonate luggage
- Shell must not crack at corner joints
- Elastic recovery expected — shell should return to approximate original shape
- Crack propagation at corners indicates insufficient wall thickness or ABS blending
- Closure system must remain functional

### ABS luggage
- Higher deformation expected compared to PC
- Corner cracking is the primary failure mode
- Cold temperature testing (-10°C) recommended for ABS products destined for cold climate markets

## What Buyers Should Request

- Corner drop test results specifically (not just flat-face drops)
- Test documentation referencing ISTA 2A or ASTM D5276
- Results issued by a qualified testing source (third-party preferred: SGS, Bureau Veritas, Intertek)
- Temperature conditions during test (room temperature vs cold)
