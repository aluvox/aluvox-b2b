# Payment Terms

## Standard Payment Structure

| Stage | Amount | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Deposit | 30% of order value | Against proforma invoice (PI) |
| Balance | 70% of order value | Against bill of lading (B/L) |

This is the industry-standard payment structure for first orders with a new luggage OEM supplier. It protects both parties:

- **Factory**: Deposit covers raw material procurement and production setup costs
- **Buyer**: Balance payment is retained until shipping documents confirm goods are loaded and in transit

## Why This Structure Works

The 30/70 split against B/L creates natural quality leverage for the buyer. If the factory produces off-specification product, the buyer has 70% of the order value as leverage to negotiate resolution before releasing final payment. This incentive structure aligns factory and buyer interests around production quality.

## Red Flags in Payment Terms

- **100% prepayment on a first order**: Transfers all delivery risk to the buyer with no leverage for quality resolution. Not standard practice in the industry.
- **No deposit accepted**: May indicate the factory is not committed to the order or does not have the working capital to procure materials.
- **Payment to a personal account rather than a company account**: Significant fraud risk.

## Tooling Payment

Tooling fees (for new mold development) are typically paid separately from the production order:

- Tooling deposit: 50–100% before mold development begins
- Tooling balance: Due upon sample approval

**Critical**: Confirm mold ownership terms in writing before paying any tooling deposit. Many factories retain mold ownership even when the buyer pays for development.

## Currency

Standard transaction currency for international luggage OEM orders is USD. Payment is typically made via T/T (telegraphic transfer / wire transfer).
