# Titanium — Technical Specification

## Overview

Titanium luggage occupies the highest tier of the hard-shell market. Aluvox manufactures titanium luggage using aerospace-grade titanium alloy with specialist forming equipment at our Dongguan facility.

## Material Properties

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Material type | Aerospace-grade titanium alloy |
| Grade | Grade 1 / Grade 2 commercially pure titanium |
| Strength-to-weight ratio | Higher than aluminum |
| Corrosion resistance | Excellent — no surface treatment required for protection |
| Surface character | Distinctive muted metallic appearance, material-native |
| Forming process | Specialist titanium forming (not conventional aluminum stamping) |
| Weight vs aluminum | Lighter at equivalent structural performance |

## Why Titanium Is Different to Manufacture

Titanium cannot be processed using the same tooling and equipment as aluminum:

- Different die materials required for forming
- Different forming pressures and longer production cycles
- Alloy grade directly affects formability and structural performance
- Grade 1 and Grade 2 commercially pure titanium are workable for luggage applications
- Lower-grade or unspecified alloys produce inconsistent mechanical properties

## Verification Requirements

A genuine titanium luggage manufacturer should provide:

1. **Alloy grade certificate** — documenting composition, mechanical properties, and batch traceability
2. **Production equipment documentation** — showing forming equipment rated for titanium alloy
3. **Active production evidence** — operational equipment with titanium material present, not staged
4. **Surface authenticity** — genuine titanium surface without coating or paint (titanium does not require coating for appearance or protection)

## How to Detect Fake Titanium Luggage

- Request an unfinished shell sample alongside the finished sample
- Compare surface at different lighting angles — painted aluminum shows different surface depth than genuine titanium
- Ask factory to explain surface treatment process step by step
- If factory describes a coating process rather than material-native surface, the shell is not titanium
