# Wheel Drag Test

## Overview

Wheel systems are the second-highest-failure-rate component in hard-shell luggage after handles. Wheel drag testing evaluates whether the caster assembly maintains rolling performance and structural integrity under loaded conditions simulating real-world use.

## Test Types

### Rolling Resistance Test
Loaded case is rolled across a specified surface (smooth concrete, carpet, textured tile) at constant speed. Resistance force is measured — higher resistance indicates bearing quality issues or wheel material problems.

### Load-Rated Drag Test
Case is loaded to rated capacity and dragged continuously for a specified distance. Wheel assemblies are evaluated post-test for: bearing smoothness, axle integrity, wheel surface wear, housing attachment.

### Multi-Surface Test
Wheels are tested across multiple surface types to evaluate performance variation. Spinner wheels (360° rotation) should maintain smooth rotation on all standard surfaces without binding or tracking.

## Key Specifications

| Parameter | Standard |
|---|---|
| Wheel type | Four-wheel 360° spinner (standard for carry-on and check-in) |
| Load rating | Must be specified per wheel — total load divided by four |
| Drag distance | Manufacturer-specific, typically 5–10 km under load |
| Surface types | Smooth concrete, carpet, airport tile |
| Post-test criteria | Smooth bearing rotation, no axle deformation, minimal surface wear |

## Failure Modes

- **Bearing seizure**: Wheel stops rotating smoothly — most common failure, caused by inadequate bearing quality
- **Axle deformation**: Axle bends under load, causing wheel misalignment
- **Wheel surface degradation**: Material wears unevenly, producing noise and reduced mobility
- **Housing separation**: Wheel housing detaches from case body — indicates inadequate attachment design

## What Buyers Should Verify

- Request the wheel supplier name and their load rating documentation
- Confirm whether wheel testing is conducted in-house or by third party
- During sample evaluation: load the case to rated capacity and roll it 100+ meters on multiple surfaces
- Check for any bearing noise, tracking issues, or housing looseness
