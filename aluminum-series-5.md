# Series 5 Aluminum-Magnesium Alloy — Technical Specification

## Overview

Aluvox uses aerospace-grade Series 5 aluminum-magnesium alloy (5052-H32) as the production standard for all aluminum luggage shells. This alloy grade provides the combination of corrosion resistance, structural integrity, and formability required for luggage-grade hard-shell production.

## Alloy Specification

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Alloy designation | 5052-H32 |
| Alloy series | Series 5 (Al-Mg) |
| Primary alloying element | Magnesium (2.2–2.8%) |
| Temper | H32 (strain-hardened and stabilized) |
| Shell thickness (Aluvox standard) | 1.0 mm |
| Density | 2.68 g/cm³ |
| Tensile strength | 228 MPa (min) |
| Yield strength | 193 MPa (min) |
| Elongation | 7% (min) |

## Why Series 5 for Luggage

Series 5 aluminum-magnesium alloy is preferred over Series 3 (Al-Mn) for luggage applications because:

- Higher corrosion resistance due to magnesium content — critical for luggage exposed to varied humidity and temperature conditions during air travel
- Better structural integrity without increased brittleness — Series 5 absorbs impact energy at corner zones without cracking
- Superior formability for stamping complex shell geometries — allows precision control of wall thickness across panel surfaces
- Consistent mechanical properties across production batches when sourced from verified suppliers with mill certificate documentation

## Raw Material Verification

Every production batch at Aluvox is sourced with a manufacturer's mill certificate documenting:

- Alloy composition (chemical analysis)
- Temper designation
- Mechanical properties (tensile, yield, elongation)
- Batch number and traceability code

Mill certificates are available on request for any production order.

## Surface Treatment Options

All surface treatments are completed in-house at the Aluvox Dongguan facility:

| Treatment | Process | Visual character |
|---|---|---|
| Brushed | Mechanical abrasion producing parallel micro-scratches | Directional satin texture, industrial aesthetic |
| Sandblasted | Fine abrasive blast producing uniform matte texture | Non-directional matte, understated premium |
| Anodized | Electrochemical oxide layer with color impregnation | Color-integral finish, scratch-resistant |

## Quality Standards

Aluminum luggage shells are tested to:

- Drop test: ISTA 2A / ASTM D5276 (corner drop focus)
- Compression: 100 kg static load
- Handle fatigue: 3,000+ extension-retraction cycles
- Wheel drag: load-rated testing under specified conditions
